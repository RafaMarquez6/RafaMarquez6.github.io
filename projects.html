My First Go Kart

The entire Go Kart was handed down to me by my eighth grade engineering teacher, Mr. Dyer.

It was originally electric, but we stripped off all the electric parts and converted it to gas — replacing the motor and controller with a 212cc snow blower engine.

We created our own steering system using rods and a wheel from Amazon, welding the parts together with a welder funded through the Belmont Wheels Club.

We learned basic mechanics of cars such as rack and pinion steering systems, and core project building skills.

"The Punisher" (Third Go-Kart)

We built this Go-Kart in the Belmont Wheels club with my engineering teacher, Mr. Dyer.

It was purchased as a kit from Drift Hero, a store for mini vehicles.

We raised $2,564 through donations across Belmont and school outreach.

I learned teamwork, suspension systems, and electrical components.

Project Timeline: September – December 2024

Third Go Kart

I built this Go Kart outside of the Belmont Wheels club with my associate, Truman Faroni. We couldn't build within the club due to school policy (no gas engines in the building).

We built this kart using recycled parts from the first go kart, including the wheels and engine. We ordered the steering wheel and steering column online.

We made our own seats and frame from scratch using the club welder and pipe bender, and bought most of the materials from our local Ace Hardware store.

Fourth Go Kart (In Progress)

This is the newest club kart, and it will be fully electric.

It is still under construction, but it is already shaping up to be the biggest kart we've built yet.

We're pushing into new territory with the electrical systems and scale of the build. Stay tuned for updates!
